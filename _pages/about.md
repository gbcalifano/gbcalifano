Giovanbattista Califano, Ph.D., is a behavioural scientist working at the intersection of consumer psychology, food science, and experimental economics. He studies how sensory cues, AI-generated imagery, and information framing shape food perception and consumer choice.

From September 2026 he holds a [Marie Skłodowska-Curie Postdoctoral Fellowship](https://marie-sklodowska-curie-actions.ec.europa.eu/actions/postdoctoral-fellowships) at the **University of Oxford**, Department of Experimental Psychology, supervised by [Prof. Charles Spence](https://www.psy.ox.ac.uk/people/charles-spence). The project, [GASTRO-X](/projects/gastro-x/), examines crossmodal nudging and AI-assisted food decision-making under Horizon Europe grant Ares(2026)3080147.

His methodological toolkit includes discrete choice experiments, mixed logit and latent class models, WTP estimation, and PLS-SEM/CB-SEM. He maintains [STEP3](https://EconPapers.repec.org/RePEc:boc:bocode:s459182), a Stata module for bias-corrected three-step latent class analysis (BCH and ML methods), published in the *Stata Journal* 26(2), 2026.

He has published 26 journal articles, with an h-index of 11 (Google Scholar). His Oxford CRL research on AI and food imagery received coverage from the BBC, Rolling Stone, Daily Mail, and Communications of the ACM.
