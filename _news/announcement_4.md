Research on AI-generated food images (with Charles Spence) received wide media coverage: [BBC Future](https://www.bbc.com/future/article/20240823-artificial-intelligence-wants-to-tell-you-what-to-cook-but-are-the-results-edible), [Rolling Stone](https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/culture-features/ah-food-pictures-fake-delivery-recipes-1235135386), [Daily Mail](https://www.dailymail.co.uk/sciencetech/article-13183271/Fake-food-created-AI-looks-tastier.html), and later [Communications of the ACM](https://cacm.acm.org/news/ai-in-the-kitchen/) (2025).

The underlying paper — "Assessing the visual appeal of real/AI-generated food images" — is in *Food Quality and Preference* 116:105149. DOI: [10.1016/j.foodqual.2024.105149](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.foodqual.2024.105149)
